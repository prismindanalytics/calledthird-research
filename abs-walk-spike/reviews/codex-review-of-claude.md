# Skeptical Peer Review of Claude Round 1

I would not accept Claude’s H1 editorial conclusion. The report establishes that the called zone changed shape somewhere, but it does **not** establish the stronger claim that the walk story should be framed as “zone confirmed” rather than “zone changed in a mixed way.” The gap is methodological.

Start with Claude’s own summary statistics. In `claude-analysis/findings.json:8-24`, the **largest** significant H1 region is not shrinkage but a **positive** bottom patch: +21.8 pp over 15 cells and 649 pitches. The top shrink patch in `:25-38` is much thinner: -24.9 pp over 130 pitches. The total significant positive area also exceeds the negative area (`:53-54`, 0.24 sq ft vs 0.18 sq ft). If the dominant significant region is expansion and the positive area is larger, “zone confirmed” is not the obvious editorial branch.

The report’s inferential scaffolding is weaker than the prose implies. The heat-map result in `claude-analysis/REPORT.md:24-30` is based on a scan over a large 2D grid with pointwise bootstrap CIs, then a post hoc search for contiguous significant patches. That is not publication-grade multiple-testing control. The GAM result in `REPORT.md:30` and `findings.json:97-106` does not rescue this: an interaction LRT only says “shape changed somewhere.” It is fully consistent with my B2 read.

The coordinate-system issue is the largest unresolved problem. Claude explicitly says there is a supplementary normalized-z analysis (`REPORT.md:20`), but the result section never reports what it says. That omission matters. I inspected Claude’s saved normalized artifact directly in `claude-analysis/artifacts/zone_grid_norm.npz` using `reviews/codex_review_scripts/claude_h1_checks.py`. Around the same overlaid “ABS top” reference line that his plot uses, the weighted mean delta is **-0.17 pp**, with **0 of 9** cells significantly negative. Across the full human-zone-normalized box, the weighted mean is **+2.51 pp**. In other words: the top-edge shrinkage that drives the prose in `REPORT.md:26-27` does **not** survive the normalization check in any clean way. Worse, the normalized figure itself is conceptually muddled: `claude-analysis/zone_grid.py:184-193` overlays ABS standing-height shares (0.27, 0.535) inside a coordinate system defined as `(plate_z - sz_bot) / (sz_top - sz_bot)`. Those are not the same units. That figure cannot adjudicate the coordinate-system dispute.

Relatedly, Claude’s rule-book interpretation in `REPORT.md:34-38` leans on pooled medians (`findings.json:184-185`) in a context where the year-specific Statcast fields moved enormously. On called pitches in the primary window, median `sz_top` is 3.420 ft in 2025 and 3.217 ft in 2026; median zone height is 1.809 ft versus 1.594 ft. That may reflect the ABS rule change, or a schema/storage change, or both. But pooled medians are not an independent validation. They collapse the very year effect under dispute.

The causal mechanism in `REPORT.md:94-102` is also overclaimed. Claude shows that first-pitch called-strike rate fell 1.77 pp and that more PAs reached 3-0. Fine. But that is an observational count-tree fact, not evidence that adjudication caused the traffic shift. When I run a sign check on the **same 2026 locations** with the two zone models (`reviews/codex_review_scripts/claude_h1_checks.py`), the 2026 model is actually **more** strike-friendly on 0-0 called pitches than the 2025 model by **+2.48 pp**. The same sign holds overall (+1.39 pp) and at 3-0 (+5.67 pp); only 3-2 is slightly negative (-0.48 pp). So Claude’s preferred first-pitch pathway does not reconcile the disagreement. Conditioned on location, my model still says the 2026 zone moved **against** the walk direction. That means the observed 0-0 called-strike drop can be explained by pitch-location mix or pitcher behavior without granting Claude’s causal chain.

Where Claude may be right: there probably **is** a real local top-edge effect in absolute `plate_z` space, and my degree-2 surface may smooth that effect into a broader mid-zone positive region. I would concede that. But that only gets him to “mixed geometric change,” not to B1. The stronger editorial claim requires showing that the local top-edge shrinkage is robust to coordinate choice and actually explains the walk spike once pitch-location behavior is held fixed. He has not done that.

The three questions the comparison memo must resolve are:

1. Does the top-edge shrinkage survive a coordinate-consistent normalized analysis, or is it mainly an artifact of absolute `plate_z` plus year-shifted `sz_*` fields?
2. On **first pitches only**, does a location-conditioned counterfactual still move the sign the wrong way? If yes, Claude’s mechanism is descriptive, not causal.
3. After correcting for multiple testing and separating local edge effects from dominant-area effects, is B1 actually defensible, or is the synthesis simply: “the zone changed shape in absolute space, but attribution to the walk spike is negative or mixed”?

My current synthesis is the latter. Claude’s geometry is suggestive. His editorial branch is not.
