# Codex Sign-Off — 7-Hole Tax Article Draft

**Verdict:** REVISE

## Summary
The numerical core of the article is mostly faithful to the Round 1 and Round 2 artifacts: the Codex LightGBM counterfactual called-pitch result, the five reverse-direction umpire flags, the H6 energy-distance null, the H7 narrow-CI artifact, and the two cross-method hitter residuals all match the JSONs. I would defend the central "no publishable 7-hole tax" conclusion. I would not sign off on the draft as written because it still contains publication-blocking sign-convention errors: it describes reverse-direction negative called-strike effects as a "larger zone" or as "FanSided direction" in multiple places. It also names two umpires after saying the article does not name any, and it still surfaces Henry Davis despite the Round 2 convergence memo saying to drop him from publication-level names.

## Numerical claims verified
- Corpus counts: `2,101` ABS challenges, `75,681` taken pitches, and `28,579` Claude borderline taken pitches match `claude-analysis/findings.json:data`. Codex Round 2 reused `75,681` called-pitch rows and `2,101` challenges, with `26,769` borderline rows under the Codex edge definition, matching `codex-analysis-r2/findings.json:data`.
- Test 1 pooled 7-hole rate: `51.2% on n=213` matches `codex-analysis/findings.json:h1_overturn_rate_by_spot` for spot 7 (`0.511737`, `n=213`). The batter-issued `37.1% on n=89`, Wilson `[27.8%, 47.5%]`, league batter rate `45.2%`, and `8.1pp` deficit match `claude-analysis/findings.json`.
- Test 2 edge-distance claim: `1.27 inches` for both spots and KS `p=0.19` match `claude-analysis/findings.json:selection_effect_signal` (`p=0.194173`).
- Test 3 Bayesian result: `−0.17pp [−1.5, +1.2]` on `n=28,579` matches `claude-analysis/findings.json:h3_called_strike_rate_delta` (`−0.1724`, `[−1.5050, +1.1746]`).
- Test 3 Codex result: `−0.35pp [−0.39, −0.31]` matches `codex-analysis/findings.json:h3_called_strike_counterfactual_pp` (`−0.3485`, `[−0.3950, −0.3111]`). The Codex JSON labels this as `n_borderline_pitches=2767`, i.e. the actual spot-7 evaluation subset, not the full `28,579` Bayesian corpus.
- Test 4 Codex umpire flags: `5` qualifying flags, all reverse-direction and `0` pro-tax, match `codex-analysis-r2/findings.json:h4_per_umpire`. The quoted ML magnitudes `−4.47pp` and `−2.21pp` match the flagged list. The Bayesian cross-walk also checks: Brian O'Nora and Hunter Wendelstedt have positive posterior medians in `claude-analysis-r2/h4_per_umpire_results.csv`, and none of the five clears the Bayesian CrI threshold.
- Test 4 Bayesian shrinkage claim: `78` qualifying umpires and `0` flagged match `claude-analysis-r2/findings.json:h4_per_umpire`. The random-slope SD median `0.076` logit also matches that JSON.

Henry Davis is not in the Codex flagged list.

- Test 7 chase interaction: Claude's low-chase `−1.45pp [−3.98, +1.03]` and `P(negative)=0.87` match `claude-analysis-r2/findings.json:h7_chase_interaction`. - Methodology description: "LightGBM with counterfactual paired prediction + SHAP + energy-distance selection probe" matches the Codex reports and code. The bootstrap-CI critique is fair: the Codex intervals resample fitted prediction deltas while holding the GBM fixed, so they are not posterior/model-uncertainty intervals.

## Editorial concerns (non-blocking)
- The H4 Bayesian random slope is bottom-of-order vs top-of-order, with a marginal spot-7-vs-3 counterfactual reported afterward. The draft mostly discloses this through the qualifying definition and Round 3 note, but adding one explicit phrase like "using a bottom-of-order random slope for power" would make the methodology harder to misread.
- The article should keep the Codex H3 denominator straight if challenged: the ML point estimate is evaluated on the spot-7 borderline subset (`n=2,767`) from a model trained on the full called-pitch corpus; the Bayesian result is the one directly summarized as `n=28,579`.
- I did not independently verify the external `60.6%` catcher-overturn claim from the prior CalledThird article because it is outside the provided project artifacts.

## Final recommendation
Do not publish this draft as written. Ship after the sign-convention language is corrected, the umpire names are removed, Henry Davis is dropped from publication-level hitter names, and the fixed-model bootstrap caveat is tightened as above. With those edits, I would defend the ML-method claims and the comprehensive-debunk conclusion in front of a FanGraphs editor.
